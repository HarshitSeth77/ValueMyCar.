What Algorithm is used?

"Value My Car" uses Random Forest at its core to predict the price of the car and is trained on a dataset collected from Kaggle.

Why?

The reason for using Random Forest as the core model for price prediction is its reliability and strong performance across various regression tasks. During our model-building process, we evaluated several algorithms and compared their performance metrics. Random Forest consistently produced stable and accurate predictions with minimal overfitting. After fine-tuning its hyperparameters, the model showed excellent generalization performance on unseen data. You can view the Comparison Charts of all algorithms used during testing.

How?

Random Forest is an ensemble learning algorithm that constructs multiple decision trees during training and merges their results to improve predictive accuracy and control overfitting. Each tree is built on a random subset of the data and features, introducing randomness that enhances model generalization. The final prediction is made by averaging the outputs of individual trees in regression tasks or by majority voting in classification tasks. This diversity among trees makes Random Forest robust to noise and outliers.
